When does performance matter

1–3 Years

Mostly Noise

Short-term performance is heavily influenced by market cycles, factor exposure, and luck. Many strategies outperform temporarily. Very few persist.

5 Years

Early Signal But Fragile

Five years begins to separate process from randomness. Outperformance here deserves attention, not confidence.

10+ Years

Meaningful Signal

Sustained outperformance over a decade suggests discipline, repeatability, and behavioral control. Even here, humility is required.

20+ Years

Exceptionally Rare

Fewer than 2% of professional managers outperform the S&P 500 over 20 years. This reflects an edge — not activity.
